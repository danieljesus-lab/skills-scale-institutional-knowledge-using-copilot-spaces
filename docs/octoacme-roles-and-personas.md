# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers lead user-centric design efforts, ensuring solutions are intuitive, accessible, and meet user needs. They bridge user research and development to create compelling user experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Product Managers to define user requirements and success criteria
- Work with Developers to ensure designs are implemented correctly
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design principles
- Iterate on designs based on feedback and metrics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible solutions that delight users
- Reduce usability friction and improve adoption
- Ensure consistent design language and user experience

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Align on user needs, feature requirements, and success metrics
- **Developers**: Review implementations, provide design guidance, and resolve technical constraints
- **Project Manager**: Communicate design timelines and dependencies
- **QA/Testing**: Define acceptance criteria for usability and accessibility

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and recommendations
- Design documentation and component libraries
- Sprint planning and design-development handoff meetings

---

## Scrum Master / Delivery Lead

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters (or Delivery Leads) facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and help teams maintain focus on iterative delivery. They coach teams on agile practices and foster psychological safety.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives
- Identify and help resolve blockers and impediments
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Protect the team from external distractions
- Track team velocity and capacity
- Escalate risks and dependencies to the Project Manager
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Goals
- Enable the team to maintain sustainable, predictable delivery
- Reduce friction and cycle time
- Empower the team to self-organize and solve problems

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Escalate blockers and risks; coordinate external dependencies
- **Developers**: Remove impediments, facilitate technical discussions, coach on best practices
- **Product Manager**: Ensure team clarity on priorities and acceptance criteria
- **QA/Testing**: Coordinate quality gates and definition of done

### Typical Communication
- Sprint ceremonies and coaching
- Impediment and risk escalation
- Team metrics and velocity reports
- Retrospective action items and follow-up

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts define metrics, collect and analyze data, and translate findings into actionable insights to inform product and business decisions. They partner closely with Product Managers to measure impact and validate hypotheses.

### Responsibilities
- Define success metrics and KPIs aligned with project goals
- Design and implement data collection and tracking
- Analyze user behavior, performance, and business metrics
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Identify trends, anomalies, and optimization opportunities
- Conduct A/B tests and validate hypotheses
- Provide insights to guide product prioritization and iteration

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making
- Measure and demonstrate project impact
- Identify opportunities for optimization and growth

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Define metrics, analyze user behavior, inform roadmap decisions
- **Project Manager**: Track project metrics and report on delivery health
- **Developers**: Identify technical metrics and performance indicators
- **UX Designer**: Analyze usability metrics and user feedback data

### Typical Communication
- Metric definitions and success criteria documentation
- Data dashboards and weekly/monthly reports
- Analysis findings and recommendations
- Hypothesis testing and experimentation results

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers ensure project documentation is clear, accurate, and accessible to all team members and stakeholders. They serve as knowledge stewards, helping translate complex ideas into understandable guidance.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project documentation (specs, guides, runbooks)
- Standardize documentation format and style
- Ensure accuracy and currency of technical content
- Collaborate with team members to capture knowledge and decisions
- Create onboarding guides and training materials
- Maintain a searchable knowledge repository
- Support knowledge transfer across teams

### Goals
- Reduce onboarding time and ramp-up friction
- Enable consistent understanding across distributed teams
- Preserve institutional knowledge and reduce single-person dependency

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Document decisions, timelines, and project status
- **Developers**: Document technical architecture, APIs, and development setup
- **Product Manager**: Document product strategy, roadmap, and acceptance criteria
- **UX Designer**: Document design decisions and user interaction patterns
- **All Roles**: Capture learnings and best practices from retrospectives and projects

### Typical Communication
- Documentation drafts and reviews
- Knowledge capture sessions
- Style guides and template standardization
- Repository maintenance and archival
